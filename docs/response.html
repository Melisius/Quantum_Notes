Linear-Transformation¶

The combined triplet and singlet one-body operator:

\[E\left(S\right)_{rs}=E_{rs}^{\alpha}+SE_{rs}^{\beta}\]

With \(S\) being \(+\) for singlet and \(-\) for triplet. The general two-body operator can be written as:

\[e_{rstu}\left(S_{1},S_{2}\right)=E\left(S_{1}\right)_{rs}E\left(S_{2}\right)_{tu}-\delta_{st}E\left(S_{1}S_{2}\right)_{ru}\]

Now some matrices can be defined:

\[\begin{split}E^{(2)}=\left(\begin{array}{cc} A & B\\ A^{*} & B^{*} \end{array}\right)\end{split}\]
\[\begin{split}S^{(2)}=\left(\begin{array}{cc} \Sigma & \Delta\\ -\Delta^{*} & -\Sigma^{*} \end{array}\right)\end{split}\]

Here the submatricies are given as:

\[\begin{split}A=\left(\begin{array}{cc} \left\langle 0\left|\left[q_{i},\left[H_{0},q_{j}^{+}\right]\right]\right|0\right\rangle & \left\langle 0\left|\left[\left[q_{i},H_{0}\right],R_{j}^{+}\right]\right|0\right\rangle \\ \left\langle 0\left|\left[R_{i},\left[H_{0},q_{j}^{+}\right]\right]\right|0\right\rangle & \left\langle 0\left|\left[R_{i},\left[H_{0},R_{j}^{+}\right]\right]\right|0\right\rangle \end{array}\right)\end{split}\]
\[\begin{split}B=\left(\begin{array}{cc} \left\langle 0\left|\left[q_{i},\left[H_{0},q_{j}\right]\right]\right|0\right\rangle & \left\langle 0\left|\left[\left[q_{i},H_{0}\right],R_{j}\right]\right|0\right\rangle \\ \left\langle 0\left|\left[R_{i},\left[H_{0},q_{j}\right]\right]\right|0\right\rangle & \left\langle 0\left|\left[R_{i},\left[H_{0},R_{j}\right]\right]\right|0\right\rangle \end{array}\right)\end{split}\]
\[\begin{split}\Sigma=\left(\begin{array}{cc} \left\langle 0\left|\left[q_{i},q_{j}^{+}\right]\right|0\right\rangle & \left\langle 0\left|\left[q_{i},R_{j}^{+}\right]\right|0\right\rangle \\ \left\langle 0\left|\left[R_{i},q_{j}^{+}\right]\right|0\right\rangle & \left\langle 0\left|\left[R_{i},R_{j}^{+}\right]\right|0\right\rangle \end{array}\right)\end{split}\]
\[\begin{split}\Delta=\left(\begin{array}{cc} \left\langle 0\left|\left[q_{i},q_{j}\right]\right|0\right\rangle & \left\langle 0\left|\left[q_{i},R_{j}\right]\right|0\right\rangle \\ \left\langle 0\left|\left[R_{i},q_{j}\right]\right|0\right\rangle & \left\langle 0\left|\left[R_{i},R_{j}\right]\right|0\right\rangle \end{array}\right)\end{split}\]

The Hamiltonian is given as:

\[H_{0}=\sum_{rs}h_{rs}E\left(+\right)_{rs}+\frac{1}{2}\sum_{rstu}\left(rs|tu\right)e\left(+,+\right)_{rstu}\]

\(R\) is the state transfer oprators that takes \(0\) to \(n\):

\[R_{n}^{+}=\left|n\right\rangle \left\langle 0\right|\]
\[R_{n}=\left|0\right\rangle \left\langle n\right|\]

Here \(n\) have to be different from \(0\). Atlast the orbital operators \(q\) are given as:

\[\begin{split}q_{j}^{+}=\left(\begin{array}{c} E\left(+\right)_{rs}\\ E\left(-\right)_{rs} \end{array}\right)=a_{r\alpha}^{+}a_{s\alpha}\pm a_{r\beta}^{+}a_{s\beta}\end{split}\]
\[\begin{split}q_{j}=\left(\begin{array}{c} E\left(+\right)_{sr}\\ E\left(-\right)_{sr} \end{array}\right)=a_{s\alpha}^{+}a_{r\alpha}\pm a_{s\beta}^{+}a_{r\beta}\end{split}\]

Here \(r>s\). Now for the triplet response the goal is to solve the response equations:

\[\left(E^{(2)}-\lambda_{j}S^{(2)}\right)X_{j}=0\]

Here the matricies \(E\) and \(S\) are given above. \(\lambda_{j}\) is the eigenvalues corrosponding to the singlet-triplet excitation energies. \(X_{j}\) is the corrosponding eigenvectors. And:

\[\left(E^{(2)}-\lambda_{j}S^{(2)}\right)X_{j}=V^{(1)}\]

Here \(\lambda_{j}\) will corrospond to the frequency of an external perturbation. These equation can be solved by an iterative procedure, by making a linear transformation:

\[u=E^{(2)}N\]
\[m=S^{(2)}N\]

Here \(N\) is a trailvector. Now to solve the equations the trailvectors is needed. The trail vector can be considered as two vectors:

\[\begin{split}N^{c}=\left(\begin{array}{c} 0\\ S\\ 0\\ S' \end{array}\right)\end{split}\]

and,

\[\begin{split}N^{o}=\left(\begin{array}{c} \kappa\\ 0\\ \kappa'\\ 0 \end{array}\right)\end{split}\]

The orbital vector can be considered as a matrix constructed as:

\[\begin{split}K_{rs}=\begin{cases} \kappa_{rs} & r>s\\ \kappa_{sr} & r<s\\ 0 & else \end{cases}\end{split}\]

Now lets consider, the linear transformation of \(u_{(pq)}^{o}=\sum_{(rs)}E_{(pq)(rs)}^{(2)}N_{(rs)}^{o}\). \(E^{(2)}\) can be thought of as a 4x4 matrix, \(N^{o}\) as a four long vector. Now the first element from this linear transformation is found to be:

\[u_{1}^{o}=\sum_{j}\left(\left\langle 0\left|\left[q_{i},\left[H_{0},q_{j}^{+}\right]\right]\right|0\right\rangle \kappa_{j}+\left\langle 0\left|\left[q_{i},\left[H_{0},q_{j}\right]\right]\right|0\right\rangle \kappa_{j}'\right)\]

Here \((pq)=i\) and \((rs)=j\). Now this can be rewritten as:

\[u_{1}^{o}=\left\langle 0\left|\left[q_{i},\left[H_{0},\sum_{j}q_{j}^{+}\kappa_{j}\right]\right]\right|0\right\rangle +\left\langle 0\left|\left[q_{i},\left[H_{0},\sum_{j}q_{j}\kappa_{j}'\right]\right]\right|0\right\rangle\]

Here it is used that \(\kappa\) is just a number, and only one of the terms depends on \(j\). Now:

\[u_{1}^{o}=\left\langle 0\left|\left[q_{i},\left[H_{0},\sum_{j}\left(q_{j}^{+}\kappa_{j}+q_{j}\kappa_{j}'\right)\right]\right]\right|0\right\rangle\]

Now by using the definition of the one-index transformed Hamiltonian:

\[u_{1}^{o}=-\left\langle 0\left|\left[q_{i},H\left(\kappa\right)\right]\right|0\right\rangle\]

The minus is from \(\left[H_{0},\sum_{j}\left(q_{j}^{+}\kappa_{j}+q_{j}\kappa_{j}'\right)\right]=-\left[\sum_{j}\left(q_{j}^{+}\kappa_{j}+q_{j}\kappa_{j}'\right),H_{0}\right]\). Now the second element:

\[u_{2}^{o}=\sum_{j}\left(\left\langle 0\left|\left[R_{i},\left[H_{0},q_{j}^{+}\right]\right]\right|0\right\rangle \kappa_{j}+\left\langle 0\left|\left[R_{i},\left[H_{0},q_{j}\right]\right]\right|0\right\rangle \kappa_{j}'\right)\]

Following the same procedure:

\[u_{2}^{o}=-\left\langle 0\left|\left[R_{i},H\left(\kappa\right)\right]\right|0\right\rangle\]

Having \(R_{i}=\left|0\right\rangle \left\langle n\right|\), now gives:

\[u_{2}^{o}=-\left\langle i\left|H\left(\kappa\right)\right|0\right\rangle\]

The third term:

\[u_{3}^{o}=\sum_{j}\left(\left(\left\langle 0\left|\left[q_{i},\left[H_{0},q_{j}\right]\right]\right|0\right\rangle \right)^{*}\kappa_{j}+\left(\left\langle 0\left|\left[q_{i},\left[H_{0},q_{j}^{+}\right]\right]\right|0\right\rangle \right)^{*}\kappa_{j}'\right)\]

Following the same procedure and using \(q_{i}^{*}=q_{i}^{\dagger}\):

\[u_{3}^{o}=-\left\langle 0\left|\left[q_{i}^{\dagger},H\left(\kappa\right)\right]\right|0\right\rangle\]

Now the fourth term:

\[u_{4}^{o}=\sum_{j}\left(\left(\left\langle 0\left|\left[R_{i},\left[H_{0},q_{j}^{+}\right]\right]\right|0\right\rangle \right)^{*}\kappa_{j}+\left(\left\langle 0\left|\left[R_{i},\left[H_{0},q_{j}\right]\right]\right|0\right\rangle \right)^{*}\kappa_{j}'\right)\]

Gives:

\[u_{4}^{o}=\left\langle 0\left|H\left(\kappa\right)\right|i\right\rangle\]

Here it is used that, \(\left[R_{i}^{\dagger},H\left(\kappa\right)\right]=-\left[H\left(\kappa\right),R_{i}^{\dagger}\right]\). Now in summary giving:

\[\begin{split}u^{o}=-\left(\begin{array}{c} \left\langle 0\left|\left[q_{i},H\left(\kappa\right)\right]\right|0\right\rangle \\ \left\langle i\left|H\left(\kappa\right)\right|0\right\rangle \\ \left\langle 0\left|\left[q_{i}^{\dagger},H\left(\kappa\right)\right]\right|0\right\rangle \\ -\left\langle 0\left|H\left(\kappa\right)\right|i\right\rangle \end{array}\right)\end{split}\]

Now consider the linear transformation of \(u_{(pq)}^{c}=\sum_{(rs)}E_{(pq)(rs)}^{(2)}N_{(rs)}^{c}\). First:

\[u_{1}^{c}=\sum_{j}\left(\left\langle 0\left|\left[\left[q_{i},H_{0}\right],R_{j}^{+}\right]\right|0\right\rangle S_{j}+\left\langle 0\left|\left[\left[q_{i},H_{0}\right],R_{j}\right]\right|0\right\rangle S'_{j}\right)\]

It can now be introduced that:

\[\left|0^{R}\right\rangle =-\sum_{n}S_{n}R_{n}^{+}\left|0\right\rangle =-\sum_{n}S_{n}\left|n\right\rangle\]
\[\left\langle 0^{L}\right|=\sum_{n}\left\langle 0\right|S_{n}^{'}R_{n}=\sum_{n}\left\langle n\right|S_{n}^{'}\]

Using the above:

\[u_{1}^{c}=-\left\langle 0\left|\left[q_{i},H_{0}\right]\right|0^{R}\right\rangle -\left\langle 0^{L}\left|\left[q_{i},H_{0}\right]\right|0\right\rangle\]

Following the same method it can be found that:

\[\begin{split}u^{c}=-\left(\begin{array}{c} \left\langle 0^{L}\left|\left[q_{i},H_{0}\right]\right|0\right\rangle +\left\langle 0\left|\left[q_{i},H_{0}\right]\right|0^{R}\right\rangle \\ \left\langle i\left|H_{0}\right|0^{R}\right\rangle +\left\langle 0\left|H_{0}\right|0\right\rangle S\\ \left\langle 0^{L}\left|\left[q_{i}^{\dagger},H_{0}\right]\right|0\right\rangle +\left\langle 0\left|\left[q_{i}^{\dagger},H_{0}\right]\right|0^{R}\right\rangle \\ -\left\langle 0^{L}\left|H_{0}\right|i\right\rangle +\left\langle 0\left|H_{0}\right|0\right\rangle S' \end{array}\right)\end{split}\]

In a similar way the linear transformation of \(S\) can be made to find:

\[\begin{split}m^{o}=\left(\begin{array}{c} \left\langle 0\left|\left[q_{i},\hat{\kappa}\right]\right|0\right\rangle \\ \left\langle i\left|\hat{\kappa}\right|0\right\rangle \\ \left\langle 0\left|\left[q_{i}^{\dagger},\hat{\kappa}\right]\right|0\right\rangle \\ \left\langle 0\left|\hat{\kappa}\right|i\right\rangle \end{array}\right)\end{split}\]

and,

\[\begin{split}m^{c}=\left(\begin{array}{c} -\left\langle 0\left|q_{i}\right|0^{R}\right\rangle -\left\langle 0^{L}\left|q_{i}\right|0\right\rangle \\ S_{i}\\ -\left\langle 0\left|q_{i}^{\dagger}\right|0^{R}\right\rangle -\left\langle 0^{L}\left|q_{i}^{\dagger}\right|0\right\rangle \\ -S_{i}' \end{array}\right)\end{split}\]
